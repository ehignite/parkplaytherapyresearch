About the Project

Randomized crossover pilot evaluating a multimodal, OT-led program targeting mitochondrial health in infants identified as “at risk” for ASD. All families ultimately receive the intervention.

Research Aims

  • Improve biomarkers of oxidative stress, energy metabolism, and inflammation.
  • Assess developmental change (Bayley, Vineland, PDMS-2, Sensory Profile).
  • Evaluate feasibility of an OT-led, biologically-informed program.

Methods & Components

  • Red light therapy (OT-supervised sessions)
  • Deuterium-depleted water (structured intake)
  • Blue-light minimization (environmental changes)
  • Soil/digging protocol (daily outdoor earth-contact play)
  • Sound-based therapy (non-headphone, room resonance)

Selected Research & Evidence

Napoli et al., 2014 — Mitochondrial dysfunction in ASD

Many children with ASD show mitochondrial abnormalities affecting energy metabolism and development.

Hamblin, 2017 — Anti-inflammatory effects of photobiomodulation

Red/NIR light can enhance mitochondrial function and reduce oxidative stress/inflammation.

Somlyai et al., 2016 — Deuterium depletion

Deuterium-depleted environments improve mitochondrial function.

Lowry et al., 2016 — Soil microbes & stress resilience

Exposure to M. vaccae supports serotonin and resilience.

Buescher et al., 2014 — Economic impact of ASD

ASD has major lifetime and national costs, showing the importance of early intervention.

SFARI / Simons Foundation Connections
  • SFARI Gene — curated gene–phenotype resource guiding biological targets.
  • Simons Simplex Collection (SSC) — deeply phenotyped simplex families.
  • SPARK for Autism — national genetic & phenotypic ASD cohort.
  • SFARI Grants — portfolio including mitochondrial/oxidative stress and early markers.

Our pilot complements these efforts by pairing mitochondrial-related biomarkers (ATP, NAD⁺/NADH, oxidative & inflammatory markers) with standardized developmental assessments (Bayley, Vineland, PDMS-2, Sensory Profile) in an OT-led, early-intervention context.
